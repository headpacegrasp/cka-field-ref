Incident Response - 4 Steps

1

Ensure Safety

Provide first aid within your training. Call 911 for life-threatening events. Do NOT leave the client unattended.

2

Notify Admin / On-Call

Phone call required for critical incidents. Within 30 minutes. Never text-only for critical events.

3

Document in Viv

Detailed visit note same day: what happened, when, actions taken, who was notified.

4

External Reporting

Abuse/neglect: call APS immediately. Serious injury/death during services: OHCQ within 24 hours.

Notification Timelines
Critical - Phone Notify Within 30 Min
  • Client fall with injury or suspected injury
  • Suspected abuse, neglect, or exploitation
  • Client unresponsive or significantly altered
  • Medication error or client elopement
  • Any event requiring 911 activation
Significant - Notify Within 4 Hours
  • Client fall WITHOUT apparent injury
  • Missed visit to high-risk client
  • Client/family safety complaint
  • Equipment malfunction affecting safety
Minor - Document Within 24 Hours
  • Missed visit to non-high-risk client
  • Minor schedule disruption
  • Non-safety verbal complaints
  • Minor property damage

Every-Visit Checklist (Viv)

  • 1
    Review Service Plan Before starting - check assigned tasks, safety notes, engagement goals
  • 2
    Clock In on Arrival Viv mobile app - records time and location (EVV)
  • 3
    Complete Service Plan Tasks Follow assigned tasks - use Interactive Caregiving approach
  • 4
    Write Visit Notes Before End of Shift Tasks completed, client status (appearance, mood, mobility, appetite), any changes from baseline, medication reminders given, engagement activities
  • 5
    Clock Out at Departure Never clock in/out for another employee
Documentation Tips Be objective and factual. Describe what you observe, not clinical interpretations. Write "client reported feeling dizzy and held onto the counter" - not "client had a hypotensive episode." Late notes (>24 hrs) require an explanation for the delay.
Every visit MUST be documented before end of shift - no exceptions.

You Are a Mandatory Reporter

This is a legal obligation under Maryland law. If you have REASONABLE SUSPICION of abuse, neglect, or exploitation of a vulnerable adult - you MUST report immediately. Failure to report is a criminal offense. You do not need proof. You do not need permission.
What to Watch For
Physical Abuse: Unexplained bruises, burns, fractures, restraint marks, fear of specific individuals
Neglect: Poor hygiene, malnutrition, dehydration, unsafe living conditions, untreated medical issues
Self-Neglect: Inability to manage personal needs, hoarding, refusal of necessary care
Exploitation: Unusual financial activity, missing belongings, sudden changes to legal documents, pressure from others
Emotional Abuse: Threats, intimidation, isolation, humiliation, controlling behavior by others
Report To: APS Anne Arundel: 410-421-8400 | APS Statewide: 1-800-917-7383 | Then notify Admin/On-Call immediately. Internal reporting does NOT replace legally required external reporting.
